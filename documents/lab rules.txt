Maintain a clean, safe, and productive environment by following these 20 essential school computer lab rules:
🖥️ Equipment CareHandle with care: 
1. Treat keyboards, mice, and monitors gently; never force cords or bang on the keys. 
2. Clean hands only: Wash or sanitize hands before using computers to prevent sticky residue on screens and keyboards.
3. No food or drinks: Keep all snacks, water, and sodas away from workstations to protect equipment from spills. 
4. Leave cables alone: Do not touch, unplug, or tamper with any wires, routers, or power cables.
5. Report damage: Notify the teacher immediately if you notice broken equipment or see sparking wires.
🔒 Security & Systems No unauthorized downloads: 
6. Do not download games, software, or files without permission from your instructor.
7. Keep passwords secret: Never share your login credentials, and always log out when you finish.
8. Do not change settings: Avoid altering the desktop wallpaper, screensavers, or system configurations.
9. Use approved websites only: Only visit educational websites and programs approved by your teacher.
10. Scan external drives: Always get teacher permission and virus-scan USB flash drives before plugging them in.
🧑‍🏫 Classroom Conduct
11. Ask to enter: Wait for the teacher's permission before entering the lab or turning on any computers.
12. Use assigned seating: Sit only at the specific computer station assigned to you by your instructor.
13. Keep voices down: Maintain a quiet, focused atmosphere and speak in a low voice so others can concentrate.
14. Eyes on the speaker: Stop typing and pay full attention when the teacher or a guest is addressing the class.
15. Respect others' privacy: Never look at, alter, or delete another student’s files.
⚡ Work Habits & Exit
16. Save your work frequently: Routinely save your projects to prevent data loss.
17. Ask before printing: Only print documents with explicit teacher approval to avoid wasting paper.
18. Use headphones: If you are listening to audio, plug in your own headphones and keep the volume at a reasonable level.
19. Follow cyber safety: Report any instances of cyberbullying or inappropriate content to your teacher right away.
20. Proper shutdown:Always shut down your computer properly, push in your chair, and clean up your workspace before leaving.
